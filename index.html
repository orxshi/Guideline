Guideline for Freshman Engineering Students

Important websites

  • Student Portal to check you advisor, course grades, etc.
  • Engineering Portal to look into exam dates, course schedule, engineering staff, academic calendar, course curriculum, etc.
  • E-learning to access online resources such as course slides, assignments, online courses.

Contents

Registration

Who is my advisor?

  • Visit student portal.
  • Log in with your student number and password (student number).
  • Click My Information tab.
  • Click My advisor to know your advisor.
  • See the faculty secretary in case of a problem.

Can I do registration myself?

  • Even though you can register courses via the student portal, you still need your advisor`s approval to complete registration.
  • Therefore, see your advisor face to face for approval.

Registration period

  • Check out academic calendar for registration period.
  • Make sure you register before registration deadline to avoid late registration penalty.

Student Email Address

  • You will need student email address to access online resources.
  • Visit student services.
  • Enter your surname with uppercase letters and student number and click send button.
  • You will be provided with your student email address and password.
  • In case of a problem, visit first floor of Smart Science building to get your student email address.

Online resources

e-learning is useful to access online resources such as assignments and exams and also attending online classes and quizzes.

Logging in

  • Visit e-learning website.
  • Click Log in in upper right corner.
  • Use student email address and password to log in.

How to enroll in a course

  • Visit e-learning website and log in.
  • Scroll down and click Faculty of Engineering.
  • Search for name of the course you want to enroll in.
  • In this example, name of the course is TFL101.
  • Click Enroll me button.

Should I take TFL101 or TURK001?

  • International students take TFL101 and TFL102 while national students take TURK001 and TURK002.

How do I attend TURK001 class?

  • TURK001 and TURK002 is an online course.
  • Follow these courses on e-learning system.

What is the schedule of TFL101 and TFL102

  • Their schedule will be announced soon.
  • Follow announcements boards for updates.

Courses

List of all courses

  • Visit departmental schedules.
  • Choose your department.
  • You will see list of all department courses.
  • You will also see course prerequisites. For example, you have to get at least D for PS111 to take PS112.

Credits of courses

  • Most of the courses are worth 3 credits. Some of them, such as MT111, are worth 4 credits.
  • Higher the credit of a course, the more influential it is on your CGPA.
  • Some of the courses such as TFL101 or TURK001 are non-credit courses, meaning that are worth no credits.
  • However, non-credits courses are still compulsory, that is, must be passed in order to graduate.
  • Non-credit courses are free of charge only for the first enrollment.

Course descriptions

  • Visit engineering catalogue, if you want to get more information about courses.

Attendance

  • Officially, you have to attend 75% of a course, otherwise, you will get NG (Nil Grade).
  • Coefficient of NG is 0.00 as F but NG is worse than F since you also lose the right to take resit and graduation make-up examinations.

Time table

  • See your advisor for time table.

Information about the classroom/time/lecturer of a course

  • Go to Complete Time Table in Engineering Portal.
  • As an example, enter eng101 in the box and click Course Code.
  • The box highlighted in red will show you course code, group number, initials of the lecturer, and classroom.

Information about staff

  • Go to Staff in Engineering Portal to find initials, email address and time-table of staff.

How do I check out exam grades?

  • Visit student portal and log in.
  • Click My Courses tab.
  • Click Current Grades to know your grades.

What type of examinations are there?

  • Midterm and final examinations are regular examinations.
  • You can take make-up examination which is replacement of midterm exam as long as you provide medical proof for your excuse of absence.
  • You can repeat final exam if get F. This type of examination is called resit exam.
  • There are also graduation make-up exams which can be taken by students about to graduate.

How successful am I?

  • After every semester, your Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is calculated based on your letter grades.
  • The courses with higher credits will have more weight on your CGPA.
  • Your CGPA is calculated out of 4.0. For example, if you get A from all of your courses your CGPA will be 4.00 out of 4.00.

How do I graduate?

  • Each course has a credit. You will earn the credit by passing a course.
  • Once you earn enough credits, you fulfill one the requirements to graduate.
  • Visit specific department in departmental schedules to see required credits to graduate.
  • For example, for Computer Engineering, you have to earn 132 credits.
  • Keep your CGPA above 2.0. Students who plan to do Master and PhD degrees are encouraged to have at least 3.0 CGPA (2.5 for some universities) on graduation.

Attending exams

  • You need exam cards to attend midterm/final/make-up/resit exams.
  • Get your exam card from faculty secretary.
  • Please read Exam Rules and Regulations.

How do I follow announcements?

  • Visit Engineering Portal frequently for announcements such as exam dates.

How should I study?

  • Follow a textbook related to the course along with slides if available.
  • If your lecturer follows a textbook, find the PDF of the textbook and follow it.
  • If your lecturer does not follow a textbook, find the PDF of the textbook that is related to the course.
  • Study daily and avoid procrastination. Starting studying a week before any examination is a very risky strategy.
